Lois Wille discusses the many problems at Crane High School

BROADCAST: Dec. 14, 1965 | DURATION: 00:55:05

Synopsis

Lois Wille caused an uproar with the story she wrote for the Chicago Daily News, "Inside a Slum High School." According to Wille's investigation, a lack of money, over crowding of students, lights that don't work in the school and no books were among some of the problems that Wille found at Crane High School. Students also had a pessimistic view, explained Wille, as she found students didn't believe the teachers and counselors cared what theyd do after they got out of high school but they just wanted them to get out and leave Crane. Some parents were willing to volunteer and help at the school, but due to legal issues, their assistance was turned away.
